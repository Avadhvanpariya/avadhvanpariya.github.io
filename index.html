Life Insurance

Health Insurance

If the policyholder becomes sick or is involved in an accident requiring hospital treatment, the insurance provider promises to pay the medical expenses incurred

Critical Illness Insurance

Medical crises such as heart attacks, stroke, or cancer are covered by critical illness insurance.such an emergency, this policy covers the extra expenses incurred

Term Life Insurance

Term life insurance is a form of life insurance that ensures the sum of a defined benefit to a nominee if the insured person
passes away

Car Insurance

Car insurance is an excellent way to safeguard your car from unexpected risks such as accidents, fire, natural calamities, or third-party liabilities

Two-Wheeler Insurance

Two-Wheeler insurance is coverage that protects your motorbike/two-wheeler from damages caused by an accident, natural calamity

Family Floater Plan

It is a health insurance policy covering a single annual premium for the entire family. It is less expensive than purchasing individual insurance in the names of family members

Group Health Insurance

This type of insurance, often known as corporate health insurance or employee health insurance, protects a group of employees who work for the same
company

Savings Plan

These investment plans provide a methodical approach to saving and building a corpus, which provides consistent returns that enable you to achieve your
future goals

Business Insurance

It safeguards businesses against damages done by unanticipated occurrences. This type of insurance aids in the protection of a company's financial resources

Travel Insurance

Travel insurance covers the expenses and damages related to traveling. It can assist a traveler in dealing with various worries and situations that might reduce their finances

Accidental Insurance

A Personal Accidental Insurance Policy covers unanticipated situations such as physical injury, general disability caused by an accident

In India, FD have remained a popular investment option. Various corporates and NBFC’s, gather and deposit money for a set period and pay interest similar to banks. These are referred to as corporate FD’s. Corporate FDs are riskier than bank FDs but offers better returns
